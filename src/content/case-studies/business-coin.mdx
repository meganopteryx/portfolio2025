## Introduction

The idea for the business card coin came to me while in an interview prep course. I imagined feeling nervous in an interview and thought "wouldn't it be nice to have something in my pocket to fidget with?" Then I got the idea to create a 3D printed business card, with a QR code for my website URL. I could idly run my hand on the textures of the coin even when just waiting for a chance to hand them out. In my mind, this seemed immediately better than a normal business card; I always ended up losing those or having them buried in a stack of papers on my desk, never to see the light of day. This would be much more eye-catching and memorable, and help establish my professional presence in a unique way. So, I set to work in the Bambu Studio program that came with my 3D printer.

##  Methodology

First, I liked having a coin shape: it was durable and easy to hold: there's a reason people have used coins for millennia. Plus, it would feel like something of value. Round coins are also great at distributing forces along their outer edges, making it likely to be much more durable than a square chip or something like that. I had to try it out so I quickly got an SVG of my logo and the QR code that links to my website and assembled a prototype in Bambu Studio with a default cylinder and recessed SVG shapes. The first challenge was to get the QR code to be readable by my phone's camera, so I set the top and bottom layers in a different color than the recessed layers.

## Version 1

The initial prototype revealed promising results. I created a cylinder shape and inset the SVGs for the logo on one side and QR code on the other. I have an AMS so I can print multiple colors in the same print, so I set the dividing line for the colors to be the height of the inset. It worked out well and my phone could read the QR code fine. 

I had been planning to add some texture or knurling to the outer edge but I would have to ask around and see if people already liked the smooth outer edge. I wasn't sure if the outer edge would be best with a gear shape or just some ribbing. So, it was time to test that out. I initially wanted to make a gear, and I found a customizable gear program for OpenSCAD. Well, it turned out that if I wanted a functional gear it would just have basically knurling anyway, so I went with it. (It's not supposed to be a functional gear. I think if I decided on a gear shape after all I'd make the base shape in a simpler vector program like Inkscape or Illustrator. Chalk that up to lessons learned.)

## Version 2 - More Texture

<Figure style={{float: 'right', 'margin-left': '.5rem', width: '100%', 'max-width': '400px'}} caption="Some coins in various colors, with knurling">
  <ResponsiveImage src="/case-studies/business-coin/PXL_20250910_190038694.webp" alt="" style={{width: '100%', height: 'auto'}}/>
</Figure>

The second iteration addressed these concerns. The coins print well and they're nice to hold. I appreciated the extra texture the knurling provided, and it made them a bit less slippery (I had dropped the first coin several times). The texture of the knurling complemented the texture of the 3D print in general, making it seem like a cohesive intentional design.

Time to get feedback. I asked my family what they thought. Initial reactions were encouraging, though my husband offered valuable insight: "How are people without phones going to know this is for Meganopteryx Design?" His observation was astute. I didn't have the text anywhere on the coin. This required revision.

<Figure style={{float: 'left', 'margin-right': '.5rem', width: '100%', 'max-width': '300px'}} caption="Text wrapped around the outside rim of the coin. The text proved illegible at this scale.">
  <ResponsiveImage src="/case-studies/business-coin/PXL_20250910_190151581.webp" alt="" style={{width: '100%', height: 'auto'}}/>
</Figure>

I had an idea that I could add the text as the texture to the outside rim, but it turned out to be basically unreadable, especially with the different color banding. So, I moved to my trusty SVG editing tool to try out some fonts and layouts.

<Figure style={{float: 'right', 'margin-left': '.5rem', width: '100%', 'max-width': '300px'}} caption="A couple of different fonts on the logo text of the coin">
  <ResponsiveImage src="/case-studies/business-coin/PXL_20250910_190231831.webp" alt="" style={{width: '100%', height: 'auto'}}/>
</Figure>

I ended up going with a circular seal-like design with the words Meganopteryx Design and meganopteryx.com as the text. I tried a few fonts, as shown below. Neither of the first two really read correctly. I ended up going with Noto Sans since it printed the best at this size. There were some other settings to tweak, such as how many layers to use for the surface to fully conceal the underlying color change, as well as shrinking the vertical height of the coin so I could put more of them in a stack.
